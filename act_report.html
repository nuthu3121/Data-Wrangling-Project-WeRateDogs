Report: act_report¶

This report presents communication of the insights and display the visualizations produced from the wrangled data. In particular, we aimed to address the following insights:

  1. Among the breed of dogs based on the third prediction, what was the main sources of favorite count?

  2. Among the breed of dogs based on the third prediction, what are the top 5 liked dogs?

  3. Among the breed of dogs based on the third prediction, is there a relationship between favorite count and retweet count?

  4. Among the breeds of dog based on the third prediction, what dog stage were the most retweeted?

We also plotted a pie chart to visualize the percentage composition of the dog stage.

Insight 1: Among the breed of dogs based on the third prediction, what was the main sources of favorite count?¶

Based on the analysis, the main sources of favorite counts among the breeds of dogs in the third prediction was iphone, followed by web client and then, tweetdeck. This implies most of the people who liked the breeds of dogs were iphone users.

Insight 3: Among the breeds of dogs based on the third prediction, is there a relationship between favorite count and retweet count?¶

To get insights into whether there is a relationship between favorite count and retweet count, we used the third prediction to filter only the breeds of dogs. We then visualized the relationship using a scatter plot. The results showed that there was a strong positive linear relationship between favorite count and retweet count. This implies that as the favorite counts increases, the number of retweets increases. That is, the most liked dogs were more likely to be retweeted and vice versa.

Insight 4: Among the breeds of dog based on the third prediction, what dog stage were the most retweeted?¶

To gain insights into what stage of dog were the most retweeted, we filtered the breeds of dogs based on the third prediction and visualized the retweet count among the four dog stages. The results revealed that dogs in the doggo stage were the most retweeted and possibly the most liked dogs. They were closey followed by the puppo and floof. However, dogs in the pupper stage were the least retweet dogs, and possibly the least liked dog stage.

We plotted a pie chart to understand the distribution of dog stages in the clean data. However, the smallest percentage of the dogs (2.4%) were from the floof stage.
